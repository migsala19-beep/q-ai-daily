2. 图结构驱动的LLM推理增强。"Visual Graph Scaffolds"论文提出新视角：图的价值不仅是测试时的外部知识源，更在于为模型提供结构化推理支架。相比传统将图作为RAG输入，该方法将图结构内化到推理过程中，有望提升复杂逻辑、数学和因果推断能力。这与FunASR在工业语音领域的突破形成呼应——当基础模型能力见顶时，通过领域专用架构（语音的流式处理、推理的图约束）实现差异化优势，正成为AI落地的关键路径。
English
Two key directions stand out:

First, memory architecture innovation for embodied AI. The AURA paper introduces "Action-Gated Memory" to address a critical gap: datacenter-optimized KV-caches are ill-suited for long-horizon, non-resetting robot episodes. While datacenter inference amortizes attention across batched short requests, embodied agents require sustained reasoning on single extended episodes. AURA's action-gated mechanism maintains constant VRAM, providing essential infrastructure for edge-deployed robot learning. This signals a broader divergence in LLM infrastructure—moving from general-purpose datacenter stacks toward specialized edge/embodied configurations.

Second, graph-structured LLM reasoning enhancement. The "Visual Graph Scaffolds" paper offers a paradigm shift: graphs serve not merely as external knowledge sources at test time, but as intrinsic structural scaffolds for reasoning. Rather than simply feeding graph data via RAG, this approach internalizes topological structure into the reasoning process itself, promising advances in complex logic, mathematics, and causal inference. Together with FunASR's industrial speech breakthrough—achieving 170x realtime performance through domain-specific architecture—these developments highlight a maturing trend: as base model capabilities plateau, competitive advantage increasingly comes from specialized structural innovations tailored to domain constraints.
