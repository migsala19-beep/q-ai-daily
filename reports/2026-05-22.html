English
Two major trends emerge from today's feed: the shift toward Data-centric AI and the productionization of Document AI.

On the Data-centric front, the paper "Let's Develop Data Probes" proposes systematic "Data Probes" to quantify how data affects LLMs across training, tuning, alignment, and in-context learning. This signals an industry pivot from model scaling to data quality engineering. Complementing this, the "Learn-by-Wire" paper introduces LBW-Guard, a bounded autonomous control framework that stabilizes training under aggressive learning rates and scale—directly addressing the rising costs and compute waste of large model training. Together, they indicate that the next phase of LLM competition will center on data engineering and training governance.

On the Document AI front, the paper "Operationalizing Document AI" presents a microservice architecture combining OCR and LLM pipelines for production scale. This aligns with the simultaneous GitHub trending of changedetection.io and containerd: AI applications are moving beyond demos toward sustainable, operations-grade systems, requiring upgrades in both infrastructure and AI service layers.
