English
Today's arXiv preprints highlight a deepening shift toward multi-agent systems and agentic planning. "When Does Personality Composition Matter for Multi-Agent LLM Teams?" investigates how personality prompting affects team-level task outcomes, suggesting that well-composed agent personas can improve collaborative LLM performance. Complementing this, "Internalizing the Future" proposes a unified agentic training paradigm that equips LLMs with world-model planning capabilities, enabling "what-if" reasoning for long-horizon decision making. On GitHub, NVIDIA/Megatron-LM remains trending, underscoring continued infrastructure demand for large-scale transformer training. Meanwhile, SimpleX Chat and VeraCrypt climbing the rankings signals growing developer and user concern for privacy and security. Finally, DysLexLens illustrates the social value of low-resource LLMs in educational accessibility, focusing on dyslexic learners.
