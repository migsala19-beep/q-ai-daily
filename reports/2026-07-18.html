English
Two clear trends emerge. First, AI agents are moving beyond software into the physical world. The paper "SPINE: Bridging the Cyber-Physical Gap with Agentic AI" argues that while foundation models give robots strong decision-making, deploying them on physical platforms still requires expert calibration, and the work aims to bridge this cyber-physical gap. Meanwhile, n8n, a fair-code workflow platform with native AI and 400+ integrations, shows that software-agent integration is becoming accessible. Second, data provenance and observability are becoming critical AI infrastructure. "OriginBlame" proposes record- and token-level data provenance for training datasets, directly relevant to copyright compliance, unlearning, and data governance. PostHog on GitHub also emphasizes unified AI observability, session replay, experiments, and logging. Together, these trends point to a future where agents are both grounded in real environments and fully observable.
