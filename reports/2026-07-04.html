English
The clearest trend is AI agents moving from general chat toward vertical task execution. Agent4cs uses a multi-agent system to summarize large hierarchical codebases, addressing the limitation of single large models in complex structures. Auto-FL-Research applies agentic search to federated learning algorithms, automating the exploration of optimizers and aggregation rules. This suggests multi-agent systems are becoming practical in B2B code understanding and research automation. A second trend is the search for new efficient small language model architectures. Wiola claims no structural lineage to GPT, LLaMA, or Mistral, building an SLM from first principles, which matters for edge deployment and cost-sensitive applications. Meanwhile, PACE advances counterfactual explainability, and the popularity of developer infrastructure projects like PostHog and Supabase shows the AI ecosystem expanding from the model layer into tooling and governance.

English
1. Add multi-agent codebase summarization to developer tools to reduce comprehension costs for large projects. (Validated by Agent4cs)
2. Evaluate new SLM architectures like Wiola for low-cost edge AI deployments. (Transformer is not the only path)
3. Add counterfactual explanation modules to high-stakes AI products to help users understand decisions. (PACE provides a usable framework)
4. Introduce difficulty-routed control in customer-service SaaS so AI handles simple issues and humans take complex ones. (Suggested by When Should Service Agents Reconsider?)
5. Watch AI application infrastructure like Supabase and PostHog as they become default backends for AI-native products.
