English
Two converging trends stand out. First, generative AI is shifting from visual plausibility to physical correctness. PhyDrawGen tackles systematic hallucinations in physics diagrams (e.g., incorrect force vectors), while the Physically Viable World Models paper argues embodied AI must represent physical structure for intervention queries, not just predict observations. This is critical for robotics simulation and engineering design. Second, financial AI infrastructure is maturing fast. Both Microsoft Qlib (now with RD-Agent for automated R&D) and OpenBB (a financial data platform for AI agents) are trending, signaling that AI-driven quant tools are moving from research notebooks to production-grade pipelines.
