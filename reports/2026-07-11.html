English
Today's arXiv papers converge on two themes: agent reliability and reasoning efficiency. AgentLens introduces production-assessed trajectory reviews for interactive coding agents, moving beyond binary pass/fail to audit decision quality, error recovery, and user experience along the entire trajectory. This signals a maturation from outcome-only metrics to full-process quality assurance. Meanwhile, "When Does In-Context Search Help?" offers a sampling-complexity theory of reflection-driven reasoning, clarifying when iterative search and self-critique genuinely help and when they merely increase cost. This can guide developers in designing multi-step reasoning and self-correction strategies. On GitHub, whisper.cpp remains active, underscoring continued interest in on-device speech AI; Bun and Vite reflect ongoing focus on JavaScript runtime and frontend build performance.
