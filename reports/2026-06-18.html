Two key trends emerge from today's signals. First, **agentic search test-time scaling is shifting from brute-force parallelization to intelligent initialization**. The paper "Beyond Parallel Sampling: Diverse Query Initialization for Agentic Search" demonstrates that standard parallel sampling yields diminishing returns when scaling search breadth, identifying query diversity as the critical bottleneck. This suggests the next competitive frontier for search agents will be "smarter first steps" rather than "more paths," complementing the practical engineering guidance in openai/openai-cookbook.

Second, **AI is embedding into professional workflows with real-world constraints rather than remaining general chat**. In legal tech, "When Rules Learn" introduces a self-evolving agent that dynamically adapts retrieval rules to handle complex legal language and precise lexical alignment. In manufacturing, two papers (SkillChain-Gym and Skill-Constrained MPC) model human skill decay and reskilling time as hard constraints within production-inventory control. This signals AI is beginning to optimize under dynamic resource limitations rather than merely generating text recommendations.
