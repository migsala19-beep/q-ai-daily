趋势二：扩散语言模型（DLM）挑战自回归范式。传统LLM逐token生成存在顺序依赖与效率瓶颈。论文《Diffusion Language Models: An Experimental Analysis》通过系统性实验评估了扩散模型文本生成，为非自回归路径在质量、速度及可控性上的权衡提供实证数据，标志着语言生成架构向多元化演进。
English
Trend 1: Agentic AI governance and vertical adoption. LLM-powered autonomous agents that invoke tools, manipulate data, and coordinate across organizations introduce severe security and compliance risks. The paper *Deontic Policies for Runtime Governance of Agentic AI Systems* proposes runtime deontic policies to dynamically constrain agent behavior, bridging the gap between design-time safety and runtime loss of control. Meanwhile, DeXposure-Claw applies an agentic system to DeFi risk supervision, demonstrating high-value financial use cases. Coupled with the trending n8n workflow platform offering native AI and 400+ integrations, agentic AI is moving from labs to production.

Trend 2: Diffusion Language Models challenge the autoregressive paradigm. Traditional LLMs suffer from sequential dependency and latency bottlenecks. The paper *Diffusion Language Models: An Experimental Analysis* provides systematic empirical evaluation of diffusion-based text generation, offering data on the quality-speed-controllability trade-offs of non-autoregressive approaches. This signals a diversification of language generation architectures beyond single-paradigm dominance.
