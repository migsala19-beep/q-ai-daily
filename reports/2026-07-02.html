今日研究热点集中在LLM Agent的自我优化与成本控制两个维度。一方面，多轮交互与反馈机制成为焦点：BayesBench评估LLM在多轮证据积累下的信念轨迹，揭示模型是否能像贝叶斯推理一样逐步校准信念；《What Drives Interactive Improvement from Feedback?》区分真正有效的反馈与单纯重采样带来的虚假提升；《Contrastive Reflection for Iterative Prompt Optimization》提出对比反思，以迭代方式优化检索Agent的prompt。另一方面，成本意识显著增强：《When Does Learning to Stop Help?》研究推理模型中的早期退出策略，在准确率与推理成本之间做动态权衡。开源侧，YOLOv5与Stable Baselines3仍保持热度，说明计算机视觉与强化学习基础组件需求稳定；Siyuan等隐私优先、自托管的知识管理工具也值得关注。整体来看，AI正从“大力出奇迹”转向更精细的效率、可解释性与Agent自优化。这些研究对构建更可靠、更经济的Agent系统具有直接指导意义，未来可能落地到客服、研究助手、代码辅助等场景。
English
Today's research highlights two interrelated directions: self-improving LLM agents and cost-aware reasoning. On the feedback front, BayesBench tracks LLM belief trajectories across multi-turn evidence accumulation, testing whether models update beliefs in a Bayesian-like manner. What Drives Interactive Improvement from Feedback? disentangles genuine feedback gains from mere resampling effects. Contrastive Reflection for Iterative Prompt Optimization uses contrastive reflection to iteratively optimize prompts for retrieval agents. These works push LLM agents toward more reliable, interpretable self-improvement. On the efficiency side, When Does Learning to Stop Help? investigates early exits in reasoning models, dynamically trading accuracy for inference cost. This is a direct response to rising API expenses and latency concerns. Meanwhile, open-source stalwarts such as YOLOv5 and Stable Baselines3 remain trending, showing sustained demand for reusable CV and RL components. Privacy-first tools like Siyuan also reflect growing interest in self-hosted, controllable knowledge systems. Overall, the field is moving from brute-force scaling toward finer-grained efficiency, explainability, and agent self-optimization.
