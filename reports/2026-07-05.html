English
Today's feed contains no new models or papers, yet GitHub Trending clearly points to one trend: self-hosting and localization of AI applications are moving from niche needs to mainstream demand. Both immich (self-hosted photo/video management) and memos (self-hosted notes) are trending, showing that users and enterprises increasingly value data sovereignty and hesitate to hand core data to closed SaaS. For AI products, whether the data layer can be privatized will directly impact compliance, trust, and long-term adoption. Tailscale's popularity offers zero-config private networking for secure remote access to self-hosted services, while Trivy makes vulnerability scanning of containers, Kubernetes, and code repositories a standard CI/CD step—an essential security layer in AI delivery pipelines. Meanwhile, frameworks like Refine lower the barrier to building internal tools, data dashboards, and B2B apps, enabling smaller teams to wrap model capabilities into clickable products. Overall, as foundation models become more commoditized, competitive differentiation is shifting from model accuracy toward data control, flexible deployment, security compliance, and fast time-to-product.

4. 用 Refine 等低代码框架快速搭建内部 AI 数据后台。理由：降低落地门槛，让模型能力变成可点击的工具。
5. 关注本地/边缘模型部署方案，准备"断网可用"的 AI 产品。理由：自托管趋势正从工具层向模型层延伸。
English
1. Migrate personal/team knowledge bases to self-hosted tools like Memos to reduce SaaS data-leak risk. Rationale: data sovereignty is the foundation of compliance and trust in the AI era.
2. Connect Tailscale to self-hosted AI services for secure remote access. Rationale: zero-config VPN simplifies private networking and keeps model endpoints from being exposed.
3. Integrate Trivy into CI/CD to scan container images and dependency vulnerabilities. Rationale: AI delivery chains are long; shifting security left prevents production incidents.
4. Use low-code frameworks like Refine to quickly build internal AI data dashboards. Rationale: it lowers the barrier to turning model capabilities into clickable products.
5. Watch local/edge model deployment options to prepare offline-capable AI products. Rationale: the self-hosting trend is extending from tooling to the model layer itself.
