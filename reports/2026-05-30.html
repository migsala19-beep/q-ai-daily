English
Today's arXiv sees two deep studies on off-policy RL prediction, showing the field evolving from "can converge" to "converge faster and more stably." Behavior-Induced Mirror-Prox TD (arXiv:2605.28849) optimizes auxiliary-variable metric geometry to accelerate off-policy prediction, while Behavior-Aware Auxiliary Corrections (arXiv:2605.28855) further regularizes covariance correction in single-timescale recursion—potentially resolving training instability from offline data reuse in production. Also notable is The Cognitive Categorical Transformer (arXiv:2605.28864), which implants category-theoretic inductive biases into a 306M-parameter GPT-2 Small backbone with cognitively inspired components. This signals LLM architecture innovation is shifting from pure scaling competition toward precision design driven by mathematical structures and cognitive theory.
