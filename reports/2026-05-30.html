English
The highlight lies in **cognitive-science-driven language model architecture** and **continued refinement of RL fundamentals**.

First, *The Cognitive Categorical Transformer* (CCT) infuses category theory and cognitive science into a GPT-2 Small backbone, yielding a 306M-parameter architecture. Category theory—mathematics' language for structural mapping—provides strong compositional inductive biases. While current LLMs rely on massive data scaling, CCT extracts prior structure from human cognition, potentially opening a path toward data-efficient AI. Though evaluated only under matched-step conditions, its interdisciplinary methodology is noteworthy.

Second, two related RL papers address off-policy TD instability: *Behavior-Induced Mirror-Prox TD* optimizes auxiliary-variable geometry via Mirror-Prox, while *Behavior-Aware Auxiliary Corrections* further regularizes covariance correction atop TDC/TDRC. This signals a shift from novel algorithm design toward deeper optimization theory—convergence guarantees under function approximation remain unsolved in real-world off-policy settings.
