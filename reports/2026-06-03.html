**方向二：Agent进入科学发现的高维决策场景**

论文 *Agents on a Tree* 将多Agent协调应用于多目标分子优化——一个在巨大化学空间中面对冲突目标的经典难题。作者指出早期设计决策会强约束后续结果，而现有单策略或固定标量化方法难以应对。通过树形路径协调机制，Agent可在早期探索阶段就考虑下游约束。这与DeepSpeed同时上榜形成呼应：上层需要更智能的Agent协调算法，底层需要更高效的分布式训练框架，两者共同推动AI for Science的工业化落地。
English
**Trend 1: Multi-Model/Multi-Agent Collaboration Protocols**

Two arXiv papers signal a shift from monolithic models to distributed intelligence. The *Consilium Protocol* paper borrows Byzantine Fault Tolerance (BFT) to treat inter-model disagreement as epistemic signal, assigning engineered cognitive personas to language models for structured deliberation. Meanwhile, *Deliberative Curation* addresses governance of shared knowledge ecosystems among stateless agents, arguing that traditional platform mechanisms fail for agent collectives. The core challenge in 2026 is evolving from "how to make an agent act" to "how to make a collective of agents reach reliable consensus."

**Trend 2: Agents in Scientific Discovery**

*Agents on a Tree* applies multi-agent coordination to multi-objective molecular optimization, where early design decisions strongly constrain downstream outcomes in vast chemical spaces. Single-policy or fixed scalarization approaches fall short; the proposed tree-based pathwise coordination lets agents account for downstream constraints during early exploration. This pairs with DeepSpeed's presence on Trending: smarter agent coordination at the application layer and efficient distributed training at the infrastructure layer together accelerate industrial AI-for-Science deployment.
